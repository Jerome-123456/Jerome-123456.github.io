Drugs

Why Do People Use Drugs?

  • Curiosity and Experimentation � Especially among young people, the desire to try something new.
  • Peer Pressure � Influence from friends or social groups.
  • Stress Relief � Coping with anxiety, depression, or difficult life situations.
  • Escapism � To escape from reality or emotional pain.
  • Recreation and Pleasure � Seeking euphoria or a temporary high.
  • Addiction � Continued use due to physical or psychological dependence.
  • Cultural or Social Norms � In some settings, drug use is normalized or even expected.
  • Medical Reasons � Using prescribed drugs that can lead to misuse.

  • Avoid Triggers � Stay away from people, places, or situations that encourage drug use.

What Can Drug Addiction Lead To?

  • Health Problems � Damage to the brain, heart, liver, and other organs; increased risk of overdose or death.
  • Mental Health Issues � Depression, anxiety, paranoia, and psychosis.
  • Relationship Breakdown � Conflict with family and friends; isolation and loneliness.
  • Poor Academic or Work Performance � Dropping out of school, losing jobs, and reduced productivity.
  • Financial Problems � Spending large amounts of money on drugs; possible debt or theft.
  • Legal Trouble � Arrests, criminal records, and imprisonment.
  • Addiction to Other Substances � Increased likelihood of abusing alcohol or other drugs.
  • Loss of Control � Inability to stop using despite harmful consequences.

Common Drugs and Their Effects

Drug Effects
Alcohol Slows brain activity, causes relaxation and lowered inhibitions; long-term use can damage the liver.
Cannabis (Marijuana) Causes relaxation, altered perception, memory issues, and slower reaction time.
Cocaine Increases energy and alertness; may cause euphoria, anxiety, and heart problems.
Ecstasy (MDMA) Boosts mood, empathy, and energy; can cause dehydration, overheating, and emotional lows after use.
Heroin Causes intense euphoria and pain relief; highly addictive and dangerous.
LSD (Acid) Creates hallucinations and mood swings; effects are unpredictable and can be frightening.
Methamphetamine (Meth) Increases energy and focus; long-term use leads to severe mental and physical health problems.
Prescription Opioids Relieve pain but can cause addiction, drowsiness, and overdose when misused.
